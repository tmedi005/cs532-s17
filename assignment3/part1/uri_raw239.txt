class="share-end"></li></ul></div></div></div><p>Over the offseason there has been a buzz surrounding the free agents that may (or may not) be returning to New England for more rings. Danny Amendola is one of the bigger names on that list, but lucky for Patriots fans around the globe, it looks like Amendola <a href="https://twitter.com/DannyAmendola/status/832799581037543424/photo/1">will agree</a> to a pay cut. The incentive is to remain with the most powerful NFL franchise over the last 15 years.</p>
<figure id="attachment_2349222" style="width: 245px" class="wp-caption alignright"><a href="http://isportsweb.com/wp-content/uploads//2017/02/USATSI_9860769.jpg"><img class="wp-image-2349222 size-medium" src="http://isportsweb.com/wp-content/uploads//2017/02/USATSI_9860769-245x320.jpg" alt="New England Patriots" width="245" height="320" srcset="http://isportsweb.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/USATSI_9860769-245x320.jpg 245w, http://isportsweb.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/USATSI_9860769-768x1001.jpg 768w, http://isportsweb.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/USATSI_9860769.jpg 800w" sizes="(max-width: 245px) 100vw, 245px" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Feb 5, 2017; Houston, TX, USA; New England Patriots wide receiver Danny Amendola (80) warms up before Super Bowl LI against the Atlanta Falcons at NRG Stadium. Mandatory Credit: John David Mercer-USA TODAY Sports</figcaption></figure>
<p>Danny has had quite the offseason so far. The thirty-one year old Texas native accepted the third pay cut of his career, draft dodging another potential three year career turmoil (e.g. St.Louis). Danny also spent some time as a <a href="https://www.boston.com/sports/new-england-patriots/2017/02/14/watch-danny-amendola-strutted-down-a-runway-at-new-york-fashion-week">runway model</a> in the ever famous New York Fashion Week.</p>
<p>But the biggest offseason buzz came from the <a href="http://www.sbnation.com/nfl/2015/2/1/7923079/super-bowl-2015-rings-winner-loser">five-million dollar repentance</a> fee that the league was forced to give Amendola and the rest of the justly-inflated league of legends. Amendola proved that somewhere between the broken glass pane of controversy in today&#8217;s NFL (cough: Darrelle Revis, Ray Rice, the Steelers version of deflategate, A.P., Shady McCoy&#8217;s 20-cent tip, the list goes on), there is still a level of personal decency in the league we&#8217;ve all had to love.</p>
<p>In the wise words of Albert Brooks &#8220;The NFL owns a day of the week, the same day the church used to own. Now it&#8217;s theirs.&#8221; But it is our fault. We the People gave Sunday to a pack of money-grubbing New-York elitists who leak more than a pasta colander. I mean seriously, between the domestic violence scandals, lack of attention to medical issues like CTE and steroid usage, and the fact that <a href="http://theheckler.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/GoodellPinocchio.jpg">this toolbag</a> made $35 million last year, I would start a petition for Robert Kraft to start his own league. No Vince McMahon b.s. either.</p>
<p>We need to stop treating the NFL like it has the same power as the MLB, because it doesn&#8217;t. The &#8220;commissioner&#8221; is a walking punch-line as well. Boo hoo, did a t-shirt <a href="http://bleacherreport.com/articles/2692504-roger-goodell-nfl-execs-reportedly-angry-with-patriots-post-super-bowl-antics">hurt your feelings</a>, Rog? It is sad that Brady&#8217;s ever-growing trophy collection will never weigh up to the commish&#8217;s burnt ego.</p>
<p>At this point what ammo does the NFL really have to hold on to? Regular season ratings are in the basement, the referees miss/over judge calls, and Pat McAfee retired in his prime to become a comedian. In other words, NFL executives should be more worried about internal difficulties than Matt Patricia&#8217;s wardrobe selections.</p>
<p>In conclusion, Danny Amendola is a Patriot and an example for front-end exec&#8217;s and players throughout the league. The Patriots have shown time and again that hard work, selflessness, and loyalty, are the keys to success. With TB12 expected to play another six years and Coach Belichick invited to stick around as Head Coach and GM of the Patriots as long as he pleases, I wouldn&#8217;t be surprised to see Danny happily agree to return to Patriot Place, and many more of his New England brothers follow along in his contractual footsteps.</p>
