						<div class="articleBody" itemprop="articleBody"><p>Ever wonder what an NFL quarterback is looking at when he takes a peak at his wrist band?</p>
<p>Well, here&#8217;s a shot of Tom Brady&#8217;s play-call wrist band courtesy of <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/nfl/comments/4n858n/tom_bradys_onfield_playcall_sheet_from_pats_hall/" target="_blank">Reddit user /u/advillious by way of the Patriots Hall of Fame</a>:</p>
<div class="wp-caption alignnone"><img class="" src="http://i.imgur.com/kkZi3r9.jpg" width="1536" height="2048" /><p class="wp-caption-text">Imgur.com</p></div>
<p>And this is why quarterbacks get paid so much money &#8212; they are tasked with memorizing all of this code and knowing exactly what it means for the other 10 players on the field.</p>
<p>Pretty overwhelming, right? Let&#8217;s break down what these calls signify.</p>
<p>Let&#8217;s go with play No. 45: &#8220;G [Gun] BROWN RT [Right] 74 HOSS X-FOLLOW&#8221;</p>
<p>Those six components establish the formation, the pass protection, and the routes the receivers are to run.</p>
<p><u><strong>Formation:</strong></u> &#8220;Gun&#8221; tells the QB to line up in shot gun, in the Patriots system &#8220;Brown&#8221; is an empty formation (no running backs) with three receivers to one side and two to the other and &#8220;right&#8221; tells the tight end what side of the formation to line up on.</p>
<p><strong><u>Protection</u></strong>: &#8220;74&#8221; signifies a five-man, empty protection. The video below goes into more detail. </p>
<p><strong><u>Routes:</u></strong> &#8220;HOSS&#8221; is a combination of a short hook route (or a &#8220;hitch&#8221;) and a vertical route down the seam. <strong>H</strong>itch + <strong>S</strong>eam = <strong>H</strong>OS<strong>S</strong>. And &#8220;X-FOLLOW&#8221; is a combo with the slot receiver running an out route and the outside receiver (the &#8220;X&#8221; receiver) running a &#8220;follow&#8221; route, which, in this case, is an in-breaking route.</p>
<p>Here&#8217;s the play drawn up:</p>
<p><img data-attachment-id="597787" data-permalink="http://ftw.usatoday.com/2016/06/tom-brady-play-call-wristband-nfl-new-england-patriots/hossxfollow" data-orig-file="https://usatftw.files.wordpress.com/2016/06/hossxfollow1.png?w=1000&#038;h=536" data-orig-size="1000,536" data-comments-opened="0" data-image-meta="{&quot;aperture&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;credit&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;camera&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;caption&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;created_timestamp&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;copyright&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;focal_length&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;iso&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;shutter_speed&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;title&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;orientation&quot;:&quot;0&quot;}" data-image-title="hossxfollow" data-image-description="" data-medium-file="https://usatftw.files.wordpress.com/2016/06/hossxfollow1.png?w=1000&#038;h=536?w=300" data-large-file="https://usatftw.files.wordpress.com/2016/06/hossxfollow1.png?w=1000&#038;h=536?w=1000" src="https://usatftw.files.wordpress.com/2016/06/hossxfollow1.png?w=1000&#038;h=536" alt="hossxfollow" width="1000" height="536" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-597787" srcset="https://usatftw.files.wordpress.com/2016/06/hossxfollow1.png 1000w, https://usatftw.files.wordpress.com/2016/06/hossxfollow1.png?w=640&amp;h=343 640w, https://usatftw.files.wordpress.com/2016/06/hossxfollow1.png?w=300&amp;h=161 300w, https://usatftw.files.wordpress.com/2016/06/hossxfollow1.png?w=768&amp;h=412 768w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></p>
<p>Further complicating matters for the quarterback, certain receivers will convert their routes based on the coverage the defense is playing. So on this particular play &#8212; one of 45 he has to internalize &#8212; Brady has to be aware of five receivers running seven different routes while diagnosing the defensive play-call &#8230; and he has only three seconds to process all of this information before a 300-pound man drives him into the dirt.</p>
<p>Playing quarterback in the NFL is hard.</p>
