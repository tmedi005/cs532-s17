										<!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
<p>TROY, N.Y. &gt;&gt; Harry Gully, the face of Lansingburgh Pop Warner and the Troy Patriots for more than 45 years, died Monday at the age of 82 according to the organization.</p>
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
<p>&#x201c;He loved teaching and coaching all of your children, and the Troy Patriots was an extension of his own family,&#x201d; the Troy Patriots posted on Facebook. &#x201c;He was proud of this organization, and it kept him going through his own family struggles. He was loved by many, but will be missed by all. The Lansingburgh community, as well as many Pop Warner programs throughout the area, were much better because of Mr. Gully. For all that Mr. Gully did, we simply say, &#x2018;Thank you.&#x2019;&#x201d;</p>
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
<p>The Lansingburgh organization formed in 1962 but Gully didn&#x2019;t get involved until 1965. More than four decades &#x2014; and thousands of players &#x2014; later, he was still helping out in any way he could. </p>
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
<p>&#x201c;Winning and losing, to me, it doesn&#x2019;t matter,&#x201d; Gully told a Record reporter in 2009. &#x201c;It doesn&#x2019;t mean anything in the long run. Giving the (opportunity) of playing, sportsmanship, meeting new people, making friends, helping kids in school ... I wouldn&#x2019;t care if we never won a game as long as these kids learned something about growing up and being a good person. Then if you do get the (big) win, that&#x2019;s like whipped cream on top of the ice cream.&#x201d;</p>

<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
<p>Troy High varsity football coach Bob Burns and former Catholic Central coach Mark Galuski played for Gully. Both remembered the Troy Patriots uniforms being nicer than their high school uniforms, and how Gully would help kids get cleats if they didn&#x2019;t have them and couldn&#x2019;t afford them. </p>
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
<p>Burns texted several childhood friends this morning that he hadn&#x2019;t talked to in years. He expects wake and services to be one of the biggest turnouts ever in Lansingburgh.</p>
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
<p>&#x201c;There&#x2019;s one thing in particular that I remember him telling me that I still tell to my kids this day,&#x201d; Burns said. &#x201c;Whenever you&#x2019;re going to make a decision to choose to do something right or wrong &#x2014; just think, if your mother was standing there next to you, would you still make the same decision. I still use that line when I coach today. That&#x2019;s something I remember him saying to me when I was 12-years-old.&#x201d;</p>
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
<p>Galuski remained very close with Gully, who had dementia. He visited him in the hospital. And he also introduced Gully when he was inducted into the Albany football Hall of Fame.</p>
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
<p>&#x201c;As soon as I started playing football, I fell in love with the game,&#x201d; Galuski said. &#x201c;I think it&#x2019;s a tribute to how Mr. Gully handled things and what he taught us as young guys. Back in those days, you got pressed. There was a lot more of the corporal discipline, a lot more military-type discipline. But he was always a guy, that, no matter what happened, he was always a supporter.&#x201d;</p>
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
<p>Gully also made an impact outside of sports. Gully and his wife, Marilyn played the part of Santa and Mrs. Claus since their marriage. The couple decorated their front lawn, at 644 Fifth Ave. in Lansingburgh, in anticipation of hundreds of children and their families who faithfully turned out to ask for presents each year.</p>
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
<p>A wake celebrating Gully&#x2019;s life is scheduled for Friday from 3-7 p.m. at McLoughlin &amp; Mason Funeral Home located at 3rd Ave and 109th St. in Troy. A funeral mass will be Saturday morning at 9 a.m. at St. Augustine Church.  </p>
<p>
