<p><b>Patriots' Day</b> (officially <b>Patriots' Day</b> in Massachusetts and Wisconsin and <b>Patriot's Day</b> in Maine<sup id="cite_ref-KenneJourn_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KenneJourn-1">[1]</a></sup>) is an official <a href="/wiki/State_holiday" class="mw-redirect" title="State holiday">state holiday</a> commemorating the anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Battles_of_Lexington_and_Concord" title="Battles of Lexington and Concord">Battles of Lexington and Concord</a>, the first battles of the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>. The holiday was originally celebrated on April 19, the actual anniversary of the battles (fought in 1775).</p>
<p>Since 1969, it has been observed on the third Monday in April in <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and in <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> (which <a href="/wiki/Missouri_Compromise" title="Missouri Compromise">until 1820</a> was part of Massachusetts). The Monday holiday creates a three-day <a href="/wiki/Long_weekend" title="Long weekend">long weekend</a>. It is also the first day of a vacation week for public schools in both states and a school holiday for many local colleges and universities, both public and private.</p>
<p>The day is a <a href="/wiki/Public_school_(government_funded)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public school (government funded)">public school</a> observance day in <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Florida law also encourages people to celebrate it, though it is not treated as a public holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Observances and re-enactments of the battles occur annually at <a href="/wiki/Lexington_Green" class="mw-redirect" title="Lexington Green">Lexington Green</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lexington,_Massachusetts" title="Lexington, Massachusetts">Lexington, Massachusetts</a> (around 6:00 am) and the <a href="/wiki/Old_North_Bridge" title="Old North Bridge">Old North Bridge</a> in <a href="/wiki/Concord,_Massachusetts" title="Concord, Massachusetts">Concord, Massachusetts</a> (around 9:00 am). In the morning, mounted re-enactors with state police escorts retrace the <a href="/wiki/Paul_Revere#midnight_ride" title="Paul Revere">Midnight Rides</a> of <a href="/wiki/Paul_Revere" title="Paul Revere">Paul Revere</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_Dawes" title="William Dawes">William Dawes</a>, calling out warnings the whole way.</p>
<p>The biggest celebration of Patriots' Day is the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Marathon" title="Boston Marathon">Boston Marathon</a>, which has been run every Patriots' Day since April 19, 1897 to mark the then-recently established holiday, with the race linking the <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athenian</a> and American struggles for liberty<sup id="cite_ref-Atlantic_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlantic-6">[6]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Marathon" title="Marathon">marathons</a> being so named after the Greek <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Marathon" title="Battle of Marathon">Battle of Marathon</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Patriots%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Patriots' Day was first proclaimed in Massachusetts in 1894 by Gov. <a href="/wiki/Frederic_T._Greenhalge" title="Frederic T. Greenhalge">Frederic T. Greenhalge</a> replacing <a href="/wiki/Fast_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Fast Day">Fast Day</a> as a public holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-KenneJourn_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KenneJourn-1">[1]</a></sup> It was established on April 19, commemorating the date of the <a href="/wiki/Battles_of_Lexington_and_Concord" title="Battles of Lexington and Concord">Battles of Lexington and Concord</a> in 1775, and consolidating the longstanding municipal observances of Lexington Day and Concord Day. It also marked the first bloodshed of the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_riot_of_1861" title="Baltimore riot of 1861">Baltimore riot of 1861</a>, during which four members of the Massachusetts militia were slain and 36 injured. The dual commemoration, Greenhalge explained, celebrated "the anniversary of the birth of liberty and union." In 1938, with the generation that had fought in the Civil War largely off the voter rolls, the Massachusetts legislature passed a bill establishing the holiday "in commemoration of the opening events of the War of the Revolution."<sup id="cite_ref-Atlantic_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlantic-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Maine followed Massachusetts in 1907 and replaced its Fast Day with Patriot's Day.<sup id="cite_ref-KenneJourn_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KenneJourn-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sporting_events">Sporting events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Patriots%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sporting events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Boston_Marathon" title="Boston Marathon">Boston Marathon</a> is run on Patriots' Day every year.<sup id="cite_ref-Atlantic_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlantic-6">[6]</a></sup> Therefore sometimes the holiday is referred to as "Marathon Monday".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> have been scheduled to play at home in <a href="/wiki/Fenway_Park" title="Fenway Park">Fenway Park</a> on Patriots' Day every year since 1959. The games were postponed due to weather in 1959, 1961, 1965, 1967, and 1984, and canceled in 1995<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> because of <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_Major_League_Baseball_strike" title="1994–95 Major League Baseball strike">the late start to the season</a>. Since 1968 the games have started early, in the morning, around 11:00 am. The early start to these games usually resulted in the game ending just as the marathon is heading through <a href="/wiki/Kenmore_Square" title="Kenmore Square">Kenmore Square</a>. However, since 2007 the marathon has started between 9:30 am and 10:00 am, resulting in the racers going through Kenmore towards the middle of the Red Sox game.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
