<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
Top Five QB's With Most to Prove at Combine
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-8305010479065135415' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://www.nfl.com/news/story/0ap3000000787152/article/five-qbs-with-most-to-prove-at-nfl-scouting-combine">Salute: NFL.com</a><br />
<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
<span style="background-color: white; color: #222221; font-family: arial, verdana, helvetica; font-size: 14px;">While all quarterbacks are under scrutiny at the combine, these five have the most to prove to make believers out of teams in need of a leader on offense.</span></blockquote>
<br />
From where I sit on this 65-degree February day in Wisconsin, it seems to me QB's have stuff to prove every day of their careers, and it only gets worse when they become the starter. &nbsp;There is no more pressure-filled job in American sports than Quarterback; and the better the QB, the worse the pressure, which is why I find it absolutely remarkable that a guy like Tom Brady, a man with unparalleled success, beautiful family, and supermodel wife, wouldn't just take his fifth Lombardi and ride off into the sunset. <br />
<br />
Anyway, none of these five guys will likely equal Brady (some won't equal Charlie Whitehurst) and they have stuff to prove.
