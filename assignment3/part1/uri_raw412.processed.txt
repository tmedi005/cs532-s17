First Family Attend Golf Club SuperBowl Party, Melania Trump In Pastels

   Feb 7
   [14]Lacey Chambers
   [15]Share

   The First Family celebrated the Superbowl in the warm confines of the
   Trump International Golf Club in Florida Sunday evening, watching the
   game with a ballroom full of other football fans and political allies.
    President Donald Trump and First Lady Melania were joined at their
   festively-decorated table by their son Barron and White House chief of
   staff Reince Priebus and his family.



   First Lady Melania Trump attended the casual Superbowl party in spring
   pastels, following the current trends.  The First Lady even made a
   daring statement with a sheer sweater top that gave a peek-a-boo
   glimpse of her nude camisole.  The effect gives the illusion of seeing
   through the outfit and catching a glimpse of forbidden undergarments
   beneath.   The style is both demure and envelope-pushing, and easy to
   emulate with a thin tunic top and fitted cami beneath.

   If you’ll notice the group outfit, each member of the entourage is clad
   in red, white, and blue.  This could be a symbolic nod to the American
   Flag, but more likely is a gesture of a support for the Patriots team,
   whose colors are the same as those of the United States.   Though
   subtle, the First Lady played on the theme with a light blue shirt,
   white pants and pumps, and a visible red sole on her heels.



   First Lady Melania paired her sheer baby blue top with slim-fitting
   white capri pants, a pair of glossy white Louboutin heels, and a
   classic white Chanel handbag. You can emulate the First Lady’s
   effortlessly chic style by choosing a glossy white pump with a dramatic
   heel.  The trademarked red sole sported by Melania is a signature of
   the Louboutin brand, but you can get the look for significantly less if
   you’re willing to compromise on the color of your soles.

   Before the game, Melania and President Trump were entertained by the
   local high school band to kick off the evening of sport.  The President
   is a long-time fan of the New England Patriots, and correctly predict
   their stunning surprise win.  The Patriots battled rival football team
   the Atlanta Falcons for the most watched and anticipated sporting event
   in America.  The winning team is traditionally flown to the White House
   to accept congratulations from the President, and this year Trump will
   do the same to his longtime friend and victorious quarterback Tom Brady
   of the Patriots.


   Attendees of the Superbowl party took to twitter with snapshots of the
   President and the First Family as they enjoyed their dinner and the
   festivities.



   The First Family sat at a roped off table in the center of the room
   where they could be visible to all assembled, as well as the press that
   were at the Golf Club covering the President’s every movement.

   The First Family left the Superbowl party early, possibly because the
   Patriots were performing so horribly no one expected them to make a
   comeback.  However, following the stunning change of tides that
   occurred in overtime and the subsequent victory for the Patriots,
   President Donald Trump sent out a tweet congratulating his team and its
   leadership.



   What did you do to celebrate the Superbowl, and did you expect the
   surprise win?  Let us know in the comments!
