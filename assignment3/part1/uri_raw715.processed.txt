Calculating Lawrence Timmons' market value

Here's Lawrence Timmons' market value as he prepares to enter the open market
for the first time.

     * [133]Bryan DeArdo - 15 hours ago
     * [134]0
     * [135]Tools
          + [136]Edit

          + [138]Slot to New England Patriots Newsletter Newsletter

   [139]Lawrence Timmons is set to test the open market come free agency.

   Last week, Steelers GM Kevin Colbert said that -- while the team would
   like to have him back for the 2017 season -- the Steelers will let
   their linebacker test the open market this offseason.

   Based on his age, productivity, and what other players of Timmons'
   caliber have recently received on the open market, what is his current
   market value?

   To find that out, Spotrac.com, an online sports website that breaks
   down salary cap and more, put together their market value for
   Pittsburgh's veteran inside linebacker. Here's the calculated value for
   Timmons this free-agent period:

   Calculated Market Value: 3 years, $18,787,050

   Avg. Salary: $6,262,350

   NFL Rank: 213 (Position Rank: 10)

   Age: 30

   Comparable Players

   Paul Posluszny: 3 years $15,000,000 (average salary:$5,000,000) -- 30
   years old when signed
   David Harris: 3 years $21,500,000 (average salary: $7,166,667) -- 31
   years old when signed
   D'Qwell Jackson: 4 years $22,000,000 (average salary: $5,500,000) --
   30-years-old when signed
   Stephen Tulloch: 1 year $2,500,000 -- 31-years old when signed
   AVERAGE: 2.8 years $15,250,000 (average salary: $5,545,455) -- 30.5
   years old when signed

   Base Calculated Value
   After adjusting the above contracts as if signed at Timmons' current
   age, we're provided with the following initial value.

   Length: 3 | Value: $17,177,778 | Avg. salary: $5,725,926

   For the full breakdown, head over to [140]spottrac.com.
   [14_5015378.jpg]

   This should give a good estimate of what the Steelers might be able to
   offer Timmons, who will test the open market for the first time in his
   11-year career. Free agency begins on March 9.
