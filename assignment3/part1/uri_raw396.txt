       <h2 class="uppercase" itemprop="headline">Martellus Bennett Favored to Return to Patriots</h2> 
       <p itemprop="description">Martellus Bennett is an unrestricted free agent and looking to cash in. Here's who's favored to sign him headed into the 2017 season, according to BetOnline.ag.</p> 
       <span itemprop="publisher" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization"> 
        <meta itemprop="name" content="sportsline.com">

       <span itemprop="articleBody"> <p>&nbsp;</p>

         <iframe id="embedVideo_1118890201805" name="embedVideo_1118890201805" src="http://www.sportsline.com/video/player/embed?args=player_id%3D1118890201805%26channel%3Dnfl%26pcid%3D882496579861%26width%3D640%26height%3D360%26autoplay%3Dtrue%26comp_ads_enabled%3Dfalse%26uvpc%3Dhttp%253A%252F%252Fsports.cbsimg.net%252Fvideo%252Fuvp%252Fconfig%252Fv3%252Fuvp_sportsline.xml%26uvpc_m%3Dhttp%253A%252F%252Fsports.cbsimg.net%252Fvideo%252Fuvp%252Fconfig%252Fv3%252Fuvp_sportsline_m.xml%26partner%3Dsportsline%26partner_m%3Dsportsline_mobile%26resizable%3Dtrue%26domain%3Dsportsline.com%26lazy_load%3Dtrue" width="640" height="360" marginwidth="0" marginheight="0" scrolling="no" frameborder="0" allowtransparency="true" allowfullscreen="true"></iframe>
        </div> <p>Almost exactly a year ago, Martellus Bennett was traded from the Chicago Bears to the New England Patriots for a fourth round draft pick.</p> <p>The result: Bennett had a career-high seven touchdowns. All that while playing alongside Rob Gronkowski, one of the league's top-producing tight ends, for a chunk of the season. He also won a Super Bowl.</p> <p>Now, he's&nbsp;an unrestricted free agent and looking to cash in.</p> <p>Here's who's favored to sign him headed into the 2017 season, according to BetOnline.ag.</p> <p><a href="http://www.cbssports.com/nfl/news/martellus-bennett-reportedly-wants-to-test-the-free-agent-market/" target="_blank"><strong>CBS SPORTS: MARTELLUS BENNETT WANTS TO TEST FREE AGENCY</strong></a></p> <p><strong>NEW ENGLAND PATRIOTS: 3/2</strong></p> <p>According to NESN, Bennett turned down a $7 million per year extension with the Patriots before last season. He's made it known that he wants to get paid and has already won a ring. With Gronkowski a perpetual health risk, the Patriots may pay Bennett to stay in Foxboro.</p> <p><strong>JACKSONVILLE JAGUARS: 5/1</strong></p> <p>The Jaguars just sent TE Julius Thomas to the Dolphins for LT Brandon Albert, leaving them with few options at the position. The Jags have $73 million in cap space, according to Spotrac, plenty of cash to sign Bennett.</p> <p><iframe class="newsletter-signup embed" src="/sportsline-web/newsletter-signup-iframe.html"></iframe></p> <p><strong>LOS ANGELES RAMS: 5/1</strong></p> <p>Lance Kendricks emerged as a capable weapon in LA last season, although he only scored twice. He's under contract through the end of next season, so we'd pass on the Rams as Bennett's next destination. They have plenty of holes to fill.</p> <p><strong>TENNESSEE TITANS: 5/1</strong></p> <p>Titans TE Delanie Walker is coming off a career-high seven touchdowns, but his reception total dipped significantly from 94 to 65. Bringing in Bennett could give the Titans another trustworthy option for QB Marcus Mariota. However, they have plenty of other needs.</p> <p><strong>TAMPA BAY BUCCANEERS: 5/1</strong></p> <p>Bucs TE Cameron Brate was a stud last season. He had more touchdowns than Bennett (eight) and hauled in 40 first downs. The Bucs paying Bennett when they have plenty of other needs, like WR and RB, seems like a poor use of cash. Still, they need more playmakers on offense in the high-flying NFC South.</p> <p><strong>DENVER BRONCOS: 8/1</strong></p> <p>The most productive tight end on the Broncos' roster last season was Virgil Green, who had just 22 catches. Bennett to Denver makes plenty of sense and they have $33 million in cap space.
