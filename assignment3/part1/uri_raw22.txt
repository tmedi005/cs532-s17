<p>Mental health professionals say their offices have been filled with patients who are depressed, anxiety-ridden, and stressed out over the results of the 2016 election. According to a CNN report this week, psychiatrists are beginning to term this new malady &#8220;post-election stress disorder.&#8221;</p>
<p>While there&#8217;s an undeniably funny side to this, some people think it&#8217;s disrespectful to the suffering many war veterans are going through on a daily basis. U.S. Rep Brian Mast, an Army vet who lost his legs to an IED in Afghanistan, says it&#8217;s a mistake to trivialize something as serious as post-traumatic stress disorder.</p>
<p>&#8220;There was a big missed opportunity in naming it &#8216;Post-Election Stress Disorder,'&#8221; he said on Fox News Tuesday. &#8220;I would have preferred they name it &#8216;Post-Inauguration Stress Disorder,&#8217; that way they could have called it &#8216;PISD.&#8217;</p>
<p>&#8220;There&#8217;s a big difference between being pissed off about things and what happens on the battlefield,&#8221; he said.</p>
<p>Mast said that while he understood that liberals were probably feeling irritated about Donald Trump&#8217;s election, psychology experts should emphasize the difference between what they&#8217;re going through and the crippling pain of PTSD.</p>

<p>“I have empathy for the stress in people’s lives in the election,&#8221; Mast said. &#8220;But that does not mean their stress has any real comparison to service members who have been targeted by snipers or blown up — who have taken the lives of their enemies or had their uniforms stained by the blood of their friends — or who have had to bury friends year after year after year. There’s not a comparison here.&#8221;</p>
<p>Obviously.</p>
<p>We have to question whether this supposed mental health crisis is even real. Sure, CNN can find one or two psychiatrists willing to back up their claim. They can round up a couple of liberals who will be happy to tell them how much they&#8217;re suffering in the wake of Trump&#8217;s inauguration. But are we really to believe that a significant number of people across the country are so cut off from reality that President Trump is responsible for their mental health deterioration?</p>
<p>Maybe, but the more likely explanation is that the lying media has found yet another fake news narrative with which to make Trump look bad.</p>
<p>The sick part about it is that millions of liberals who felt just fine will read this and start thinking, &#8220;Yeah&#8230;yeah, come to think of it, I AM depressed! Cool, I have a new identity. I have a new &#8216;illness&#8217; to blame for my crappy life situation!&#8221;</p>
<p>You think any of these psychiatrists ever say, &#8220;Turn off your friggin Facebook for a day or two&#8230;?&#8221;</p>
