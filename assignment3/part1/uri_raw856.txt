<p>New England Patriots head coach Bill Belichick <a href="http://www.espn.com/nfl/story/_/id/18742276/patriots-bill-belichick-list-potential-witnesses-aaron-hernandez-double-murder-trial">may be called to testify</a> at former tight end Aaron Hernandez&#8217;s double murder trial if his name is chosen off the defense&#8217;s witness list. Offensive coordinator Josh McDaniels is also mentioned on the same list, as the defense prepares their witnesses in a 2012 case in which Hernandez has been charged with a double homicide in a drive-by shooting.  Hernandez is currently serving a life sentence since 2015 for the killing of Odin Lloyd.</p>
<p>Belichick was the only head coach Hernandez had in his three seasons in the NFL. The Patriots (and largely Belichick) drafted him in the fourth round of the 2010 NFL Draft out of Florida, despite reports that he had failed multiple drug tests while in college.</p>
<p>His best season came in 2011, when the Patriots unleashed the tandem of Hernandez and fellow tight end Rob Gronkowski in two-tight end packages, with Hernandez catching 79 passes for 910 yards and seven touchdowns. The duo own the single-season record for receptions, receiving yards, and touchdowns for tight end teammates. For his career, Hernandez finished with 175 catches for 1,956 yards and 18 touchdowns over 38 games, all of which came under Belichick.</p>
<div class="ftb-widget" data-width="800" data-height="450" data-widget-id="lT6EN9ax0H3" data-frozen="" data-href="">
	</div>

<p>Hernandez would not make it to his fourth NFL season, where he was set to begin cashing in on the $40 million contract extension he had received from New England. He was charged with first-degree murder in June, before being found guilty in April 2015 and sentenced to life imprisonment.</p>
<p>The case that Belichick (and McDaniels) would be asked to testify at represents another case entirely, one in which Hernandez is on trial for a double homicide.</p>
