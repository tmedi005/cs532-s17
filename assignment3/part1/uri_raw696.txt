		<h1 class="entry-title">Bill Belichick on witness list for second Aaron Hernandez murder&nbsp;trial</h1>
	<div class="post-info">
	Posted by Mike Florio on February 22, 2017, 10:52 PM EST</div>
	<div class="post-body clearfix">
		<span class="enclosure" style="width:242px;"><img width="242" height="212" src="https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/633940794-e1487821962271.jpg?w=242" class="featured-image wp-post-image" alt="HOUSTON, TX - FEBRUARY 05:  Head coach Bill Belichick of the New England Patriots looks on prior to Super Bowl 51 against the Atlanta Falcons at NRG Stadium on February 5, 2017 in Houston, Texas.  (Photo by Al Bello/Getty Images)" srcset="https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/633940794-e1487821962271.jpg?w=242 242w, https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/633940794-e1487821962271.jpg?w=484 484w, https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/633940794-e1487821962271.jpg?w=194 194w, https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/633940794-e1487821962271.jpg?w=311 311w" sizes="(max-width: 242px) 100vw, 242px" data-attachment-id="2443560" data-permalink="http://profootballtalk.nbcsports.com/2017/02/22/bill-belichick-on-witness-list-for-second-aaron-hernandez-murder-trial/super-bowl-li-new-england-patriots-v-atlanta-falcons-93/" data-orig-file="https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/633940794-e1487821962271.jpg" data-orig-size="3534,3095" data-comments-opened="1" data-image-meta="{&quot;aperture&quot;:&quot;5.6&quot;,&quot;credit&quot;:&quot;Getty Images&quot;,&quot;camera&quot;:&quot;Canon EOS-1D X Mark II&quot;,&quot;caption&quot;:&quot;HOUSTON, TX - FEBRUARY 05:  Head coach Bill Belichick of the New England Patriots looks on prior to Super Bowl 51 against the Atlanta Falcons at NRG Stadium on February 5, 2017 in Houston, Texas.  (Photo by Al Bello\/Getty Images)&quot;,&quot;created_timestamp&quot;:&quot;1486313940&quot;,&quot;copyright&quot;:&quot;2017 Getty Images&quot;,&quot;focal_length&quot;:&quot;800&quot;,&quot;iso&quot;:&quot;6400&quot;,&quot;shutter_speed&quot;:&quot;0.000625&quot;,&quot;title&quot;:&quot;Super Bowl LI - New England Patriots v Atlanta Falcons&quot;,&quot;orientation&quot;:&quot;1&quot;}" data-image-title="Super Bowl LI &#8211; New England Patriots v Atlanta Falcons" data-image-description="" data-medium-file="https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/633940794-e1487821962271.jpg?w=311" data-large-file="https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/633940794-e1487821962271.jpg?w=1024" /> <span class="caption">Getty Images</span></span><p>Patriot owner Robert Kraft <a href="http://profootballtalk.nbcsports.com/2015/03/31/kraft-tells-jury-hernandez-said-he-was-in-club-during-shooting/">testified in the first Aaron Hernandez trial</a>. Patriots coach Bill Belichick could testify in the second Aaron Hernandez trial.</p>
<p>Via the <em>Associated Press</em>, Hernandez&#8217;s lawyers <a href="http://www.espn.com/nfl/story/_/id/18742276/patriots-bill-belichick-list-potential-witnesses-aaron-hernandez-double-murder-trial">have added Belichick to the list</a> of possible witnesses. The head coach <a href="http://profootballtalk.nbcsports.com/2017/02/03/josh-mcdaniels-may-be-called-to-testify-in-aaron-hernandez-trial/">joins offensive coordinator Josh McDaniels</a> as persons who may take the oath and answer questions.</p>
<p>The potential purpose of Belichick&#8217;s testimony isn&#8217;t known. Typically, lawyers pump up the list with an overly broad list of names in order to conceal the actual case strategy that would arise if the lawyers listed only the witnesses who actually will testify.</p>
<p>Putting it another way, it&#8217;s like listing 20 players as &#8220;questionable&#8221; so that the other team won&#8217;t know who&#8217;s actually injured.</p>
<p>Already convicted and serving life without parole for the June 2013 shooting death of Odin Lloyd, Hernandez now stands trial for the July 2012 murders of Daniel de Abreu and Safiro Furtado. Jury selection has begun, and opening statements are scheduled for March 1.</p>
