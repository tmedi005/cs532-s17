                  <p>The Super Bowl is an enormous entertainment juggernaut, but it's also a food giant and a significant driver of restaurant business.</p> 
<p>According to&nbsp;<em>Forbes</em>&nbsp;magazine, the U.S. Department of Agriculture states that after Thanksgiving, Super Bowl LI will be the largest food consumption day in America. The big game – including the immensely popular television commercials and the much-hyped half-time show – has a similar impact in Canada.</p> 
<h2>The numbers</h2> 
<p>Statistics&nbsp;<a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/geoffwilliams/2017/01/30/1-33-billion-chicken-wings-to-be-eaten-on-super-bowl-sunday-and-more-food-factoid-fun/#745213c83811" target="_blank">cited</a>&nbsp;by&nbsp;<em>Forbes</em>&nbsp;also reveal that on Super Bowl Sunday:</p> 
<ul> 
 <li>Over one billion chicken wings will be consumed.</li> 
 <li>12 million pizzas will be eaten.</li> 
 <li>Guacamole will be made from 190 million pounds of avocadoes shipped from Mexico.</li> 
 <li>It will all be washed down with 350 million gallons of beer.</li> 
</ul> 
<p>In terms of human resources and jobs, Forbes says that in mid-January, Pizza Hut hired 11,000 additional employees, and companies delivering food can see their business increase by one-third over an ordinary Sunday. The magazine also notes that bar owners say that the Super Bowl, a day that is to restaurants what Christmas is to retailers, can account for nearly 75 per cent of food sales over the year.<br> <br> While economists agree on the economic bolster of the global event, this&nbsp;crush of bar business in the U.S. is felt in Canada too, where business owners remark that the game – this year, it's New England Patriots versus Atlanta Falcons in Houston, Texas – has a huge impact on the economy.</p> 
<p>Waterloo Region's food and drink industry will also see a boost. Jay Taylor of Morty's in Waterloo, a popular sports bar near Wilfrid Laurier University since 1981, says that the Super Bowl drives the pub's menu.</p> 
<p>"It is our busiest Sunday of the year and our biggest take-out day by far," Taylor said. "We will literally sell a ton of chicken wings on Super Bowl Sunday."</p> 
<div class="figure_full">
 <img src="https://i.cbc.ca/1.1500372.1379033924!/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/original_620/hi-wings-852.jpg" alt="hi-wings-852" srcset="https://i.cbc.ca/1.1500372.1379033924!/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/original_460/hi-wings-852.jpg 460w, https://i.cbc.ca/1.1500372.1379033924!/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/original_620/hi-wings-852.jpg 620w, https://i.cbc.ca/1.1500372.1379033924!/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/original_940/hi-wings-852.jpg 940w" sizes="(max-width:50em) 100vw, 60vw" width="100%">
 <p class="figure-caption"></p>
</div> 
<h2>East versus South&nbsp;</h2> 
<p>In addition to the usual pub and football fare, Morty's is preparing "tribute foods" of the Super Bowl cities: New England clam chowder and chicken-fried steak with spicy "Hotlanta" wings with cayenne and white gravy. Dessert is traditional American apple pie.</p> 
<p>"For the wings, we probably do two-thousand for take-out. We have to cook them in 15-minute increments," Taylor adds.</p> 
<p>At Cambridge's Gator's Tail, a pub with a Cajun flavour, chef and co-owner Alanna Sisto looks for Super Bowl Sunday to drive super sales as well.</p> 
<p>"We do about three times the sales during the Super Bowl over a regular Sunday. It's a lot of regulars and families. The bar was sold out a month ago and there's overflow that comes in. We're big New England fans here, so I'll make clam chowder," Sisto says.</p> 
<h2>Super Bowl 'tribute foods'&nbsp;</h2> 
<p>San Francisco-based food writer and editor Anna Monette Roberts also believes in comparisons between Thanksgiving and the big&nbsp;game, both in sports bars and at home.</p> 
<p>"Instead of gathering around a dinner table with your family, you're gathering around a coffee table and television with your friends. The game is the centre-piece, but the drinks and food certainly play a very large role in the Super Bowl," she says.</p> 
<p>There's little doubt that chili is one of the go-to foods for Super Bowl festivities at restaurants and bars; however, for home Super Bowl parties, Monette Roberts has created a wide variety of Super Bowl recipes that are found on&nbsp;<a href="http://www.popsugar.com/food/Super-Bowl-2017-Food-Ideas-43050078#photo-43050078" target="_blank">Popsugar.com</a>&nbsp;if you want to make them yourself at home.</p> 
<p>"For Atlanta and Georgia," she says, "the two most popular dishes are a chili-cheese dog from a drive-in restaurant called The Varsity and the Atlanta-based Chick-fil-A fried chicken sandwich served with pickles. In Boston, foods could be a lobster roll, either hot or cold, dressed with some mayo and of course clam chowder. That latter is from Union Oyster House established in 1826. People can re-create these flavours no matter where they are."</p> 
<p>In keeping with the general boom in interest in food over the last decade or so, Super Bowl food has become more deeply ingrained in American – and we can extrapolate Canadian – culture, says Monette Roberts.</p> 
<p>"Back in the day, people used to just do simple crock-pot chili," she says, adding that heightened interest and information gained from the Internet have inspired home cooks.</p> 
<p>"They're very interested in the foods from every city. Each year, people research ahead of time what the iconic dishes are from Super Bowl cities. They want to have a competition of the food and the beer."&nbsp;&nbsp;</p> 
<h2>Super Bowl grub around this&nbsp;region</h2> 
<p>In Kitchener's Eastwood Plaza,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Strykerz-Kitchen-Bar-960007810747038/" target="_blank">Strykerz Kitchen and Bar</a>&nbsp;is cooking Super Bowl chili and is having a hot wings-eating contest with a very hot Reaper pepper sauce called "Instant Regret" (the Reaper pepper – registering two million Scoville heat units – is likely the world's hottest pepper).</p> 
<p><a href="http://moosewinooskis.com/" target="_blank">Moose Winooski's</a>,&nbsp;just off Sportsworld Drive in Kitchener, says Super Bowl Sunday is super busy. Part of their menu includes a football-shaped hamburger.</p> 
<p>In northeast Waterloo,&nbsp;<a href="http://stlouiswings.com/restaurant/waterloo/" target="_blank">St. Louis Bar and Grill</a>&nbsp;will be featuring nachos and all-you-can-eat chili dogs with the purchase of a $20 ticket, which also qualifies you for a chance to win the "Super Bowl Squares" game.</p> 
<p>Get there early, because central Waterloo's&nbsp;<a href="http://www.mortys.com/index.php" target="_blank">Morty's Pub</a>, like many sports bars, will be packed. The competition on the table will be between southern chicken-fried steak and New England clam chowder.<br> <br> On Franklin Boulevard in Cambridge,&nbsp;<a href="http://www.thegatorstail.com/" target="_blank">Gator's Tail Sports Shack and Grill</a>&nbsp;will have appetizer specials and is preparing chili with andouille sausage and Cajun spices.</p> 
<p>Perhaps a little more quiet is the event at Guelph's&nbsp;<a href="http://www.borealisgrille.ca/" target="_blank">Borealis Grille and Bar</a>&nbsp;on Gordon Street, where they are nevertheless cooking up both beef and vegetarian chili was well as wings and nachos. There will also be prize draws and Super Bowl Squares.</p>
