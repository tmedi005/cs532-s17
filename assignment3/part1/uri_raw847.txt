Martellus Bennett may be headed for the exit.</p><p><br></p><p>Bennett proved to be a successful stand-in for Gronkowski this season, hauling in 55 catches along with a career high 7 touchdowns for the Patriots.</p><p><br></p><p class="" data-label="Drop here...">However, with great talent comes great responsibility. And a hefty paycheck to boot. The price may just be too much to pay to keep Bennett around.</p><i class="wrapper" contenteditable="false"><div class="tweet-container tweetEmbedded" contenteditable="false" data-tweetid="834194620674371585" data-tweeturl="https://twitter.com/RapSheet/status/834194620674371585" data-tweetauthor="RapSheet"><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr"><a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/Patriots?src=hash">#Patriots</a> TE Martellus Bennett will test free agency. </i>
<p><i class="wrapper" contenteditable="false"></i></p><p class="" data-label="Drop here...">Recently a staple in the Patriots' system&nbsp;has been small, quick receivers alongside a dominant tight end. With Gronkowski missing half the regular season, and all of the playoffs, Bennett was that dominant force.</p><p class="" data-label="Drop here..."><br></p><p class="" data-label="Drop here...">Given Gronk's recent injury history, the Patriots may hurt more than we might think if Bennett were to leave. Gronk is starting to look like his health can't be completely trusted given the fact that he's missed significant time due to injury in two of the last four seasons.<br>​</p><p class="" data-label="Drop here...">Keeping Bennett around is certainly in their best interests, but the price may just be too high.&nbsp;</p><i class="wrapper" contenteditable="false"><div class="tweet-container tweetEmbedded" contenteditable="false" data-tweetid="834194770713014274" data-tweeturl="https://twitter.com/RapSheet/status/834194770713014274" data-tweetauthor="RapSheet"><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">The <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/Patriots?src=hash">#Patriots</a> helped Martellus Bennett revive his career, and it’s a mutually respectful relationship. But keeping him in NE will be hard</p>— Ian Rapoport (@RapSheet) <a href="https://twitter.com/RapSheet/status/834194770713014274">February 22, 2017</a></blockquote></div>
</i>
<p><i class="wrapper" contenteditable="false"></i></p><p class="" data-label="Drop here...">The reality is that the Patriots usually can make things work regardless of who is or isn't on the roster. As long as they have No. 12 behind center, they always have a chance.</p><p class="" data-label="Drop here..."><br></p><p class="" data-label="Drop here...">However, the Patriots usually don't capture world championships absent of a productive tight end. If Bennett does leave, look for New England to fill the void regardless of Gronk's status.</p><p class="" data-label="Drop here..."><br></p><p class="" data-label="Drop here...">Although, after the season&nbsp;Bennett had, those will be mighty big shoes to fill.&nbsp;</p></div><div class="fanorakel"></div><div class="ad ad--dfp ad--empty" data-slot-id="1x1secondary"></div></div><section
