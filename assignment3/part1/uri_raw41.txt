	After claiming his fifth Super Bowl championship as the head coach of the New England Patriots earlier this month, Bill Belichick lamented that his franchise was already five weeks behind opponents in preparing for the 2017 season.</p>      <p>
	He may need to put down the film for another reason.</p>      <p>
	In a court filing on Tuesday, Belichick was listed as one of 122 potential civilian witnesses in the upcoming double homicide trial of Aaron Hernandez, a former Patriots tight end. Those names were provided by Hernandez’s defense team. Patriots offensive coordinator Josh McDaniels was also on the list, as were Hernandez’s former Florida Gators teammates, Maurkice and Mike Pouncey.</p>                          <div class="ra-module h" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2979624.1487799344!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_750/belichick23s-3-web.jpg">  <figure class="ra-figure">  <span class="ra-enlarge"></span>        <img srcset='http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2979624.1487799344!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_750/belichick23s-3-web.jpg 750w, http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2979624.1487799344!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_1200/belichick23s-3-web.jpg 1200w' alt='Aaron Hernandez is already serving a life sentence for the 2013 killing of Odin Lloyd.' src='http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2979624.1487799344!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_750/belichick23s-3-web.jpg' />   <figcaption>  <h2 class="ra-caption" itemprop="description">Aaron Hernandez is already serving a life sentence for the 2013 killing of Odin Lloyd.</h2>    <span class="ra-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">(Angela Rowlings/AP)</span>   </figcaption> </figure> </div>             <p>
	David Nelson, a former Gator and New York Jets receiver, is also a potential witness.</p>    <p> <a class="ra-related" href="http://www.nydailynews.com/sports/football/aaron-hernandez-shouldn-call-mike-pouncey-anymore-article-1.2979415">Maybe Aaron Hernandez shouldn't call Mike Pouncey anymore</a> </p>        <p>
	The trial is scheduled to start March 1. Jury selection is ongoing.</p>                          <div class="ra-module h" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2979627.1487799345!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_750/super-bowl-patriots-football.jpg">  <figure class="ra-figure">  <span class="ra-enlarge"></span>        <img srcset='http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2979627.1487799345!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_750/super-bowl-patriots-football.jpg 750w, http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2979627.1487799345!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_1200/super-bowl-patriots-football.jpg 1200w' alt='Bill Belichick is listed as one of a potential 122 witnesses the defense team could call.' src='http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2979627.1487799345!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_750/super-bowl-patriots-football.jpg' />   <figcaption>  <h2 class="ra-caption" itemprop="description">Bill Belichick is listed as one of a potential 122 witnesses the defense team could call.</h2>    <span class="ra-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">(Steven Senne/AP)</span>   </figcaption> </figure> </div>             <p>
	Hernandez has been charged in the killings of Safiro Furtado and Daniel de Abreu in Boston’s Theatre District on July 16, 2012. Hernandez was drafted by Belichick in 2010, and caught a touchdown pass from quarterback Tom Brady in the Super Bowl XLVI loss to the Giants five months before the double killing. Hernandez is serving a life sentence for murdering Odin Lloyd on June 10, 2013.</p>      <p>
	If Hernandez is convicted of killing Furtado and de Abreu, that would mean that he played a season with the Patriots after doing so.</p>                 <p>
	Belichick was also listed as a potential witness in the Lloyd trial. He was never called, but Patriots owner Bob Kraft testified against Hernandez, the player Kraft once rewarded with a five-year contract worth up to $40 million.</p>                       <dl
