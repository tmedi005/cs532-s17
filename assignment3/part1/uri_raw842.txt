<p>I hated&nbsp;<em>Arrival</em>&nbsp;&mdash; it just sucked. I didn&#39;t like&nbsp;<em>Fences</em>&nbsp;because they just filmed the play &mdash; I wanted to see the guy go into the jazz club and play his music, the girl who&#39;s having his baby, his kid on the football field. But I think Denzel [Washington, its producer/director/star] decided that every word of the script [by the late August Wilson] was so precious that he wasn&#39;t going to &quot;mess&quot; with it, and the movie suffered as a result. I loved the first half of&nbsp;<em>Lion</em>, but I felt like a different director and cinematographer made the second half. I thought&nbsp;<em>Hidden Figures</em>&nbsp;was wonderful &mdash; because it&#39;s a great story, not because it was especially hard to tell. It&#39;s almost like a glorified Movie of the Week.&nbsp;<em>Moonlight</em>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<em>Hacksaw Ridge</em>&nbsp;were really very good, but I don&#39;t think of them as a best picture.&nbsp;<em>La La Land</em>&nbsp;was tremendously enjoyable, but not all that deep or memorable. I think he&#39;s a different person now; he&#39;s a very talented director, and I wish him well. [<em>Moonlight</em>&#39;s] Barry Jenkins did a really good job, but the movie&#39;s three parts aren&#39;t as connected as they could have been. That left me with&nbsp;<em>Manchester</em>&nbsp;[Kenneth&nbsp;Lonergan] and&nbsp;<em>La La Land</em>&nbsp;[Damien&nbsp;Chazelle]. But I voted for&nbsp;Lonergan, because it was harder to make everything click on that movie, and he really succeeded.</p>
