        <div data-page="1" data-page-hidden="0" data-use-autolinker="false"><p><strong>HOUSTON --</strong> Generally speaking, <a href="http://www.cbsnews.com/super-bowl/" target="_blank">Super Bowl</a> pregame interviews aren&rsquo;t a great source of stimulating conversation. And yet every year, reporters gather 12-deep for this clich&eacute; fest.</p><p>Fortunately, this year there was a rookie for New England with something novel to talk about: novels, like &ldquo;Gone Girl.&rdquo;</p><figure class="image pull-left image-small "><span class="img "><img src="http://cbsnews3.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2017/02/04/02f61365-d03f-49ed-9339-07726d2ed5cc/resize/220x/1a7e4d9166ee3cce1b036a240bd1cec5/hartman-malcolm-mitchell-exported-01.jpg" alt="hartman-malcolm-mitchell-exported-01.jpg" srcset="http://cbsnews3.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2017/02/04/02f61365-d03f-49ed-9339-07726d2ed5cc/resize/220x/1a7e4d9166ee3cce1b036a240bd1cec5/hartman-malcolm-mitchell-exported-01.jpg 1x"></span><figcaption><div class="caption"><p>Malcolm Mitchell</p></div><div class="credit">
                                            CBS News
                                        </div></figcaption></figure><p>&ldquo;What about her false diary, how does the author use that in the narrative?&rdquo; I asked.</p><p>&ldquo;Well, the diary is almost a different character in the book,&rdquo; Malcolm Mitchell said.</p><p>I first met this voracious reader, wide receiver <a href="http://www.cbsnews.com/news/football-star-shows-you-cant-judge-a-book-by-its-cover/" target="_blank">three years ago</a>. Malcolm was in college then, playing for Georgia, when one day he ran into a woman at Barnes and Noble. She didn&rsquo;t know he was a famous football player and invited him to join her book club, which he did.</p><p>And that&rsquo;s how one of the top wide receivers in the country began meeting monthly with his book club lady friends.</p><p>He was the only man and the youngest by a generation. But Malcolm didn&rsquo;t care -- didn&rsquo;t care what anyone thought.</p><p>&ldquo;Somebody called me a nerd. It&rsquo;s not a word that I&rsquo;m used to hearing, &ldquo; Malcolm said. &ldquo;I was proud of it ... It&rsquo;s like a badge of honor to me, knowing where I came from.&rdquo;</p><figure class="image pull-none image-large "><span class="img "><img src="http://cbsnews3.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2017/02/04/fea03079-d13c-43a1-80c5-faff3c5b6647/resize/620x/84ffc08e3a471736557934e41e31c9e8/hartman-malcolm-mitchell-exported-01-jpg2.jpg" alt="hartman-malcolm-mitchell-exported-01-jpg2.jpg" srcset="http://cbsnews3.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2017/02/04/fea03079-d13c-43a1-80c5-faff3c5b6647/resize/620x/84ffc08e3a471736557934e41e31c9e8/hartman-malcolm-mitchell-exported-01-jpg2.jpg 1x"></span><figcaption><div class="caption"><p>Malcolm in his book club</p></div><div class="credit">
                                            CBS News
                                        </div></figcaption></figure><p>Malcolm confessed that when he started college he could only read at about a junior high level. And it bothered him. So he started putting as much effort into his reading game as his football game.</p><p>Every free moment he had a book in his hand. Until eventually, he was reading them by the dozens.</p><p>And that&rsquo;s why, no matter what he does on Sunday, Malcolm says football will never be his proudest accomplishment.</p><p>&nbsp;&ldquo;That came natural. That&rsquo;s a gift. I had to work to read,&rdquo; he said</p><p>Which brings us to the latest chapter in his life story: Now the reader is a writer, too.</p><figure class="image pull-none image-large "><span class="img "><img src="http://cbsnews3.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2017/02/04/f081657f-db86-4b0a-93bc-c61cc7113aaf/resize/620x/38897120f9f1c6a77692efbb5a798fce/hartman-malcolm-mitchell-exported-01-jpg3.jpg" alt="hartman-malcolm-mitchell-exported-01-jpg3.jpg" srcset="http://cbsnews3.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2017/02/04/f081657f-db86-4b0a-93bc-c61cc7113aaf/resize/620x/38897120f9f1c6a77692efbb5a798fce/hartman-malcolm-mitchell-exported-01-jpg3.jpg 1x"></span><figcaption><div class="caption"><p>Malcolm reading his book to school kids</p></div><div class="credit">
                                            CBS News
                                        </div></figcaption></figure><p>&ldquo;So I wrote the book that you have in your hands today, &lsquo;The Magician&rsquo;s Hat,&rsquo;&rdquo; he told a group of school kids.</p><p>&ldquo;The Magician&rsquo;s Hat&rdquo; is a children&rsquo;s book about the magic of reading. He has also started a kid&rsquo;s literacy foundation. All of which leads me to the same conclusion I had after my first meeting with Malcolm: If we could all just follow his example, the world would be in a perfectly good place.</p><p>&ldquo;You don&rsquo;t know how much that means to me man,&rdquo; Malcolm said.</p><p>Malcolm Mitchell, Super Bowl winner.</p><p><em>To contact On the Road, or to send us a story idea, email us: OnTheRoad@cbsnews.com</em>  </p></div>
