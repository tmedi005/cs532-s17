						<p><strong style="font-size: 13.008px;">By Todd Starnes</strong></p><p>Students at Dinwiddie High School in Virginia were ordered on Monday to remove American flags flying from their truck beds.</p><p>Daniel Nunnally, Jr., told television station WRIC he was pulled out of class and told to remove not only Old Glory but also the Gadsden flag.</p><p>"I kind of started getting angry because what's the problem with the American flag," Daniel told the television station.</p><p>Daniel is not the kind of kid to cause trouble, so he did as he was ordered to do -- and retired the Stars &amp; Stripes.</p><p>Word spread after reporter Parker Slaybaugh posted the story on his Facebook page and the following day more than a dozen students showed up to class with the Star-Spangled Banner proudly flying on their vehicles.</p>						<div class="more">
