<p>The New England Patriots&#8217; head coach may testify in the double-murder trial of a former player.</p>
<p><strong>FOX&#8217;s Gurnal Scott with the story:</strong></p>
<div class="smart-track-player-container  stp-color-1e73be-1e73be" data-url="http://dts.podtrac.com/redirect.mp3/ak.podcast.foxnewsradio.com/news/dotcom/BELICHICK_022217.mp3" data-background_color="#1e73be" data-image="http://radio.foxnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/FOX-News-Radio-80x80.jpg" data-download="false" data-color="1e73be" data-title="Patriots&#8217; Bill Belichick Could Possibly Testify in Aaron Hernandez Trial" data-artist="FOX News Radio" data-paid="true"  data-social="true"  data-social_twitter="true"  data-social_facebook="true"  data-social_gplus="true" data-speedcontrol="true" data-uid="1DoJBWkt" data-download_id="efb0c8fcadf6d1e43ba3e719a92e9f2e" ></div><div class="audioplayer_container"><style> .audioplayer_container {display:none;} </style></div>
<p><em>Bill Belichick, who earlier this month won his fifth Super Bowl with New England, could possibly take the stand for the defense team of Aaron Hernandez.</em></p>
<p><em>Hernandez, a former Patriots&#8217; tight end is charged in the drive-by shooting deaths of two men in 2012. In addition to Belichick, New England&#8217;s offensive coordinator Josh McDaniels could also testify. The process of selecting a jury is already underway with March 1 set as the date to begin trial.</em></p>
<p><em> Hernandez is already serving a prison sentence for the 2013 of a man dating his fiance&#8217;s sister.</em></p>
