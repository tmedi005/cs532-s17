   Martellus Bennett set to test free-agent market



   Feb
   22
         2/22/2017 10:53:33 AM
      | More
   Tight end Martellus Bennett might just be one-and-done with the
   Patriots, Chris Wesseling of [34]NFL.com reports.
   Bennett will indeed test free agency, NFL Network Insider Ian Rapoport
   reported Tuesday. Based on his asking price as the most coveted tight
   end on the market, it's a "real possibility" Bennett will play for
   another team in 2017, per Rapoport.
   Based on this news, the franchise tag will not be an option for the
   2014 Pro Bowler.
   The Patriots and Bennett have a "mutually respectful" relationship,
   Rapoport added, but it will be quite a challenge for the two sides to
   find common ground on value once the new league year begins on March 9.
   The team is understandably unwilling to pay any tight end more than Rob
   Gronkowski's $9 million annual figure.
   Soon after trading for Bennett last offseason, New England offered him
   a new deal worth $7 million per year, Rapoport reported early this
   month. Bennett promptly rejected that offer.
