Patriots 2001 Super Bowl ring, Rob Gronkowski's Deflategate gloves up for
auction

   [156]Print [157]Email
   [158]Heather Adams | Hadams@masslive.com By [159]Heather Adams |
   Hadams@masslive.com masslive.com
   [160]Follow on Twitter
   on February 22, 2017 at 1:15 PM, updated February 22, 2017 at 1:20 PM
   [161]comments

   Looking to add to your sports memorabilia collection? Lelands.com's
   winter auction has some unique New England Patriots and Boston Celtics
   collectibles.

   One of the items is [162]Rob Gronkowski's gloves he wore during
   the 2015 AFC Championship Game between the Patriots and the
   Indianapolis Colts, also commonly known as the Deflategate game. If
   that isn't enough to grab your attention, both gloves are signed.
   According to the website, the item starts $2,500.

   According to the website, the item starts $2,500.

   Lelands.com also sold the "Deflategate" ball in a previous auction for
   $43,923.

   "This is a highly unusual opportunity to own a piece of one of the
   great sports controversies of the modern era," the website says.

   Another Patriots item in the winter auction is a [163]2001 Patriots
   Super Bowl ring. The ring originally belonged to one of the longest
   tenured employees in Patriots history, George Boyajian, according to
   the website. According to the website, the item started at $5,000 but
   with 23 days left of bidding it is already past $7,000.

   If you're more of a Celtics fan, [164]Lelands.com is also auctioning
   off the 1964 Boston Celtics 7 Times World Champions Award. It was
   originally presented to Frank Ramsey, who was considered the first
   "sixth man" in NBA history. This item starts at $2,000.

   The winter auction runs until March 17.

   The most sought after sports memorabilia piece right now, however, is
   still on the run -- Tom Brady's [165]Super Bowl 51 jersey.

   But, as Josh Evans, owner and founder of [166]Lelands.com, told
   [167]MassLive in an earlier interview, that item is "unsellable."
