		<h1 class="headline">Patriots: Trey Flowers returns as an anchor at defensive end, but questions abound</h1>
				<figure class="thumb">
			<div class="image"><img src="http://www.southcoasttoday.com/storyimage/NB/20170222/SPORTS/170229775/AR/0/AR-170229775.jpg&MaxH=225&MaxW=225"></div>
			<!--div class="image-caption">
				<div class="description">Trey Flowers is coming off his best season as he gets ready for the third year of his rookie contract. ROSS D. FRANKLIN/THE ASSOCIATED PRESS</div>
				<div class="credit"></div>
			</div-->
		</figure>
		<div class="article-meta">
			<div class="inner">
				<div class="article-meta-main">
					<div class="article-meta-day">Wednesday<div>
					<span class="article-meta-date">Feb&nbsp;22,&nbsp;2017&nbsp;at&nbsp;10:08&nbsp;PM</span>
					<span class="article-meta-updated">Feb&nbsp;22,&nbsp;2017&nbsp;at&nbsp;10:08&nbsp;PM</span>
				</div>
			</div>
		</div>
		<div class="article-body">
			<p class="article-summary"></p>
			<span class="byline">
				By Mark Daniels@MarkDanielsPJ
			</span>
			<p><p>With the 2016 season in the books, the Providence Journal will take a look at each position group on the Patriots, analyzing how the team performed in 2016 and where it might be heading in the future. Today we look at defensive end.</p><p>Players: Trey Flowers, Jabaal Sheard, Rob Ninkovich, Chris Long, Geneo Grissom</p><p>Contract situation: It looks like the Patriots have an anchor here, but they&#x2019;re going to need to get younger and add some bodies to the defensive end position this season.</p><p>Flowers is coming off the best year of his career and he enters 2017 on the third year of his rookie contract. He&#x2019;s set to count for $754,733 on the Patriots salary cap this season. The 23-year-old is signed through 2018 before becoming an unrestricted free agent in 2019.</p><p>Beyond Flowers, there are a lot of questions.</p><p>Ninkovich has one year left on his contract and he&#x2019;s on the books for $2.250 million next season. At 33 years old, it wouldn&#x2019;t be surprising if 2017 was the veteran&#x2019;s last season in the NFL.</p><p>Both Sheard and Long are unrestricted free agents. Long, 31, was noncommittal when The Providence Journal asked if he wanted to play next season. If he does, he wouldn&#x2019;t cost the Patriots a lot to retain. Sheard is a different story. At 27 years old, he is in the prime of his career.</p><p>Grissom is an exclusive rights free agent and likely to be back.</p><p>2016 review: The season got off to a slow start for the Patriots edge rushers. With Ninkovich serving a four-game PED suspension, Sheard and Long started the season as the two defensive ends. The amount of quarterback sacks just wasn&#x2019;t there consistently during the first half of 2016.</p><p>That all changed thanks to Flowers. The second-year player amassed four sacks in two games and by Week 10, he was a starting defensive end. Flowers turned into the Patriots best pass rusher and finished with seven sacks &#x2014; all made in the second half of the season. His ability to rush from the inside also gives the Patriots an advantage. He blew up in the Super Bowl, finishing with 2.5 sacks.</p><p>Last season, it was mutually beneficial to have four defensive ends that could rotate in and out of each game.</p><p>Ninkovich started opposite Flowers and finished with four sacks in 12 games. Long also finished with four sacks, but his ability to get to the quarterback paid off in other ways. In Super Bowl LI, he drew a fourth-quarter holding penalty on Jake Matthews to help push the Falcons out of field-goal range. That was instrumental in the Patriots' comeback.</p><p>Sheard had an up-and-down season. After making eight sacks last season, many thought he would replace Chandler Jones this past year. Instead, Sheard made headlines when the Patriots left him at home during their trip to San Francisco. The healthy scratch was notable. Sheard eventually got on the same page with his coaches, but finished with only five sacks this season.</p><p>Grissom, a 2015 third-round pick, was cut at the end of training camp and started the season on the practice squad. He eventually made his way back on the 53-man roster and played 11 games this season &#x2014; mainly contributing as a core special teamer (250 special team snaps). He played only 12 defensive snaps in 2016.</p><p>The future: The Patriots need to get younger at defensive end. Luckily, they have Flowers to build around.</p><p>That&#x2019;s where it starts for the Patriots and at this point, Flowers looks like a rising star and could develop into the Patriots' best defensive player. The team needs to surround him with depth. Ninkovich brings great leadership, but isn&#x2019;t getting any younger and can&#x2019;t play every snap on defense.</p><p>With Long and Sheard up for new contracts, it makes all the sense in the world for the Patriots to bring in another defensive end, or two, via the 2017 NFL Draft. If the Patriots could pair Flowers with another up-and-coming edge rusher, this defense would be primed for the long haul.</p><p>It wouldn&#x2019;t be shocking to see another team offer Sheard more money than the Patriots are willing to spend since edge rushers go for a premium price. The Pats could also try to bring Long back or another veteran, who is looking to win.</p><p>The team could solve some issues if Grissom develops into the player they thought he could. After getting cut last summer, he&#x2019;s anything but a guarantee in 2017.</p></p>
