        <header>
                <h4 class="entry-title">Patriots Coach Bill Belichick Could Be Called As Witness In Aaron Hernandez Murder Trial « CBS Boston</h4>
            </header>

    <div class="elements-box">
        <div class="sc_ads_within_two"><div data-mantis-zone="420-headline-patient-category-top-of-articles"></div></div><p>   <br />
</p>
<div>
<p>BOSTON (CBS) — New evidence in the upcoming Aaron Hernandez double murder trial has been released, including transcripts from jailhouse cellphone conversations and a lengthy potential defense witness list that includes Patriots coach Bill Belichick.</p>
<p>The list contains nearly 270 names, and Belichick is listed as number 11.</p>
<p>He was also on the potential witness list when Hernandez was first tried and convicted for the murder of Odin Lloyd, but Belichick was never called.</p>
<p><a href="https://cbsboston.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/2-21-17updatedwitnesslistcommonwealth-of-massachusetts.pdf" target="_blank">See defense witness list (PDF)</a></p><div class="insert-post-ads" style="clear:both;float:left;width:100%;margin:0 0 20px 0;"><div align="center" data-mantis-zone="content-1"></div></div>
<p>“Ninety-eight percent of them will not be called,” said lawful expert Harry Manion. “What a defendant does in all trials, and especially murder one trials, is name the conceivable universe.”</p><div class="insert-post-ads" style="clear:both;float:left;width:100%;margin:0 0 20px 0;"><div data-mantis-zone="content-2"></div></div>
<p>Manion sees only a limited circumstance this time around for Belichick.</p>
<p>“The evidence may go in a way where the Commonwealth challenges that he was known to be a thug in the locker room, that he was known to be a bad guy,” said Manion. “They may bring Belichick out and say, have you heard any of that? I’m doubting the Commonwealth would do that, though.”</p>
<div id="attachment_805346" style="width: 430px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"></p><div class="insert-post-ads" style="clear:both;float:left;width:100%;margin:0 0 20px 0;"><div data-mantis-zone="content-3"></div></div>
<p class="wp-caption-text">Belichick and Hernandez (Image credits Al Bello/Getty Images and WBZ)</p>
</div>
<p>Earlier in the month, a defense motion said Patriots offensive coordinator <a href="http://boston.cbslocal.com/2017/02/03/josh-mcdaniels-aaron-hernandez-murder-trial-new-england-patriots/">Josh McDaniels may testify</a> on the possible meanings of some of Hernandez’s tattoos.</p>
<p>WBZ-TV Sports Director Steve Burton is also listed as a potential witness in the court paperwork. The documents cite Steve’s 2011 interview with Hernandez.</p>
<p>Hernandez is about to go on trial for the a double murder in Boston’s South End in July of 2012. Daniel Abreu and Safiro Furtado were both gunned down in a drive-by shooting.</p>
<p>Prosecutors contend Hernandez told a friend that one of the victims was “trying” or challenging him during an encounter at a nightclub.</p>
<p>They want to submit a jailhouse cellphone conversation where he tells a former college teammate he plans to get another tattoo in the shape of a stop sign that will send the message, “beware, no hesitations  Feel me, like no try me there’s no hesitations.”</p>
<p>In another call, prosecutors say Hernandez tells his mother she cannot be trusted with information.</p>
<p>Manion believes the calls “are a reach,” but prosecutors hope it helps get to motive in the case, a case Manion sees as a tough one for the defense.</p>
<p>“What’s the defense, somebody else did it?” Manion said.</p>
<p>According to the documents, the defense also plans to call experts to testify about marijuana use in the NFL, and the correlation between marijuana and violence.</p>
<p>Jury selection continues with the trial set to start March 1.</p>
<p>Hernandez is already serving a life sentence without parole for the 2013 murder of Odin Lloyd.</p>
