<p><b>Shizuoka Prefecture</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">静岡県</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shizuoka-ken</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Prefectures_of_Japan" title="Prefectures of Japan">prefecture</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%ABbu_region" title="Chūbu region">Chūbu region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Honshu" title="Honshu">Honshu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The capital is the city of <a href="/wiki/Shizuoka,_Shizuoka" title="Shizuoka, Shizuoka">Shizuoka</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Hamamatsu" title="Hamamatsu">Hamamatsu</a> is the largest city by population.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shizuoka_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Historic_Sites_of_Shizuoka_Prefecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Historic Sites of Shizuoka Prefecture">Historic Sites of Shizuoka Prefecture</a></div>
<p>Shizuoka Prefecture was formed from the former <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dt%C5%8Dmi_Province" title="Tōtōmi Province">Tōtōmi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suruga_Province" title="Suruga Province">Suruga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Izu_Province" title="Izu Province">Izu</a> provinces.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The area was the home of the first Tokugawa Shogun.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a> held the region until he conquered the lands of the <a href="/wiki/H%C5%8Dj%C5%8D_clan" title="Hōjō clan">Hōjō clan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kant%C5%8D_region" title="Kantō region">Kantō region</a> and placed land under the stewardship of <a href="/wiki/Oda_Nobunaga" title="Oda Nobunaga">Oda Nobunaga</a>. After becoming <a href="/wiki/Shogun" title="Shogun">shogun</a>, Tokugawa took the land back for his family and put the area around modern-day Shizuoka city under the direct supervision of the shogunate. With the creation of <a href="/wiki/Shizuoka_han" class="mw-redirect" title="Shizuoka han">Shizuoka han</a> in 1868, it once again became the residence of the Tokugawa family.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shizuoka_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Shizuoka Prefecture is an elongated region following the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Suruga_Bay" title="Suruga Bay">Suruga Bay</a>. In the west, the prefecture extends deep into the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Alps" class="mw-redirect" title="Japan Alps">Japan Alps</a>. In the east, it becomes a narrower coast bounded in the north by <a href="/wiki/Mount_Fuji" title="Mount Fuji">Mount Fuji</a>, until it comes to the <a href="/wiki/Izu_Peninsula" title="Izu Peninsula">Izu Peninsula</a>, a popular resort area pointing south into the Pacific.</p>
<p>As of 1 April 2012, 11% of the total land area of the prefecture was designated as <a href="/wiki/List_of_national_parks_of_Japan#History" title="List of national parks of Japan">Natural Parks</a>, namely the <a href="/wiki/Fuji-Hakone-Izu_National_Park" title="Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park">Fuji-Hakone-Izu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minami_Alps_National_Park" title="Minami Alps National Park">Minami Alps</a> National Parks; <a href="/wiki/Tenry%C5%AB-Okumikawa_Quasi-National_Park" title="Tenryū-Okumikawa Quasi-National Park">Tenryū-Okumikawa Quasi-National Park</a>; and four Prefectural Natural Parks.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tokai_earthquakes">Tokai earthquakes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shizuoka_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tokai earthquakes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkai_earthquakes" title="Tōkai earthquakes">Tōkai earthquakes</a></div>
<p>Throughout history, a disastrous earthquake called the Tokai earthquake has hit Shizuoka every 100 to 150 years. On Tuesday, March 15, 2011, Shizuoka Prefecture was hit with a magnitude 6.2 earthquake approximately 42&#160;km (26&#160;mi) NNE of <a href="/wiki/Shizuoka,_Shizuoka" title="Shizuoka, Shizuoka">Shizuoka City</a>.</p>
