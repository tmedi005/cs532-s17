		<h1 class="entry-title">Martellus Bennett to fans: Stop telling me to take less&nbsp;money</h1>
	<div class="post-info">
	Posted by Mike Florio on February 22, 2017, 7:08 PM EST</div>
	<div class="post-body clearfix">
		<span class="enclosure" style="width:250px;"><img width="250" height="212" src="https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/609374814-e1487808501947.jpg?w=250" class="featured-image wp-post-image" alt="FOXBORO, MA - SEPTEMBER 18:  Martellus Bennett #88 of the New England Patriots looks on before the game against the Miami Dolphins at Gillette Stadium on September 18, 2016 in Foxboro, Massachusetts.  (Photo by Tim Bradbury/Getty Images)" srcset="https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/609374814-e1487808501947.jpg?w=250 250w, https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/609374814-e1487808501947.jpg?w=500 500w, https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/609374814-e1487808501947.jpg?w=200 200w, https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/609374814-e1487808501947.jpg?w=320 320w" sizes="(max-width: 250px) 100vw, 250px" data-attachment-id="2443545" data-permalink="http://profootballtalk.nbcsports.com/2017/02/22/martellus-bennett-to-fans-stop-telling-me-to-take-less-money/miami-dolphins-v-new-england-patriots-66/" data-orig-file="https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/609374814-e1487808501947.jpg" data-orig-size="3460,2924" data-comments-opened="1" data-image-meta="{&quot;aperture&quot;:&quot;3.2&quot;,&quot;credit&quot;:&quot;Getty Images&quot;,&quot;camera&quot;:&quot;Canon EOS-1D X Mark II&quot;,&quot;caption&quot;:&quot;FOXBORO, MA - SEPTEMBER 18:  Martellus Bennett #88 of the New England Patriots looks on before the game against the Miami Dolphins at Gillette Stadium on September 18, 2016 in Foxboro, Massachusetts.  (Photo by Tim Bradbury\/Getty Images)&quot;,&quot;created_timestamp&quot;:&quot;1474202100&quot;,&quot;copyright&quot;:&quot;2016 Getty Images&quot;,&quot;focal_length&quot;:&quot;300&quot;,&quot;iso&quot;:&quot;640&quot;,&quot;shutter_speed&quot;:&quot;0.000625&quot;,&quot;title&quot;:&quot;Miami Dolphins v New England Patriots&quot;,&quot;orientation&quot;:&quot;1&quot;}" data-image-title="Miami Dolphins v New England Patriots" data-image-description="" data-medium-file="https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/609374814-e1487808501947.jpg?w=320" data-large-file="https://nbcprofootballtalk.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/609374814-e1487808501947.jpg?w=1024" /> <span class="caption">Getty Images</span></span><p>When the Patriots traded for tight end <a class="nameLink" href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nfl/4639/martellus-bennett">Martellus Bennett</a>, they knew that: (1) he had one more year left on his contract; and (2) if he and/or the team had a big year, Bennett would try to parlay the experience into a contract potentially worth more than what the Patriots would be willing to pay. Indeed, the Patriots have a habit of trading for players in the final years of their contracts because if/when the players leave via free agency, the departure counts toward the team&#8217;s eventual haul of compensatory draft picks.</p>
<p>Of course, that reality isn&#8217;t keeping some fans from lobbying Bennett to take less to stay in New England. To that, Bennett had this to say: &#8220;Stop @&#8217;ing me about taking less money. <a href="https://twitter.com/MartysaurusRex/status/834521286814613504">You take less money [at] your job?</a> All of you take a pay cut hahaha.&#8221;</p>
<p>Later, he said this: &#8220;All I&#8217;m saying is cut your own <a href="https://twitter.com/MartysaurusRex/status/834523343944634369">grass don&#8217;t be all in my garden</a> f&#8211;king with my fruit while weeds are growing all over yours.&#8221;</p>
<p>Bennett is right. Football players have a limited number of years to play, and they have every right to try to get as much as they can while they can. Owners chase every dollar they can without criticism; players should do the same.</p>
<p>Actually, players have even more reason to pursue as much money as possible because they don&#8217;t own anything other than their own bodies. At a time when management and labor are roughly splitting the revenues, the players get half the revenue and the owners get all the revenue and all the equity.</p>
<p>So go for it, Marty and every other free agent. Get paid. Use your leverage. And don&#8217;t apologize to any of the people who aren&#8217;t putting their short-term and long-term health interests on the line to engage in a profession that too many trivialize by calling it a &#8220;game.&#8221;</p>
<p>It&#8217;s not a game. It&#8217;s a multi-billion-dollar enterprise that keeps growing and growing and the men who have careers that last for a blink of an eye in relation to the overall life of the business should do anything and everything to get what they can while they can.</p>
