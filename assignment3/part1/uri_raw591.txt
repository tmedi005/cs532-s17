<p>PEMBROKE, NC &ndash; Freshman guard <a href="/sports/wbkb/2016-17/bios/Bullock_Abigail">Abigail Bullock</a> came off the bench and connected on 5-of-9 three-point attempts, including three in a game-deciding second-quarter outburst that carried Francis Marion University to its eighth straight win, a 68-52 road victory over UNC Pembroke, Wednesday night (Feb. 22) in Peach Belt Conference women's basketball action.</p>
<p>FMU concludes its regular-season schedule with a 17-11 overall mark and a 12-7 league record.&nbsp; The Patriots, winners of 10 of their last 11 contests, finish second in the PBC's East Division standings and will host the University of North Georgia on Saturday at 6 p.m. in a quarterfinal game of the Peach Belt Tournament.&nbsp; It will be only the second-ever PBC Tournament game hosted by the Patriots &ndash; joining a 2010 home win over USC Aiken.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.peachbeltconference.org/Tournaments/16-17/17basketball/20170222wbbbracket" target="_self"><strong>2017 PBC TOURNAMENT BRACKET</strong></a></p>
<p>Sophomore <a href="/sports/wbkb/2016-17/bios/fulmore_shaunice_qljb">Shaunice Fulmore</a> led Francis Marion with 20 points and six rebounds, while junior <a href="/sports/wbkb/2016-17/bios/chandler_khai_5ujh">Khai Chandler</a> scored 18 points and Bullock 15.&nbsp; Chandler joined Bullock in the three-point barrage as she nailed 4-of-8 shots from behind the arc and FMU as a team was 10-of21.</p>
<p>FMU senior point guard <a href="/sports/wbkb/2016-17/bios/flowers_tete_1wbs">TeTe Flowers</a> scored seven points, grabbed seven rebounds, and handed out 11 assists, while junior 5-10 center <a href="/sports/wbkb/2016-17/bios/tines_kristin_s906">Kristin Tines</a> led the Patriots on the boards with nine rebounds.</p>
<p>Brianna Baham paced the Braves with 14 points, while Airico Williams posted a double-double with 10 points and 13 rebounds.&nbsp; UNCP (15-11, 10-9) will enter the tournament as the East Division's third seed.</p>
<p>UNC Pembroke's only lead of the game came at 16-15 when Nyla Allen scored the final bucket of the first quarter.&nbsp; A three-pointer by Chandler to open the second period put FMU ahead to stay as the Patriots outscored the Braves 25-8 in the quarter to assume a 40-24 halftime advantage.</p>
<p>The Braves closed to within 42-29 early in the third period, but a 7-2 spurt by FMU pushed the margin back to 18 points.&nbsp; Francis Marion led 55-38 heading to the final quarter.&nbsp; UNCP trimmed the margin to 11 points on two occasions, the final time at 57-46 with 6:19 remaining.&nbsp; Fulmore scored four straight points to ignite a 9-2 run that gave FMU a 66-48 lead with one minute left.</p>
<p>Francis Marion shot 45 percent from the floor, while holding UNCP to only 28.6 percent &ndash; FMU's best defensive effort of the year.&nbsp; The Patriots also held a 24-6 edge in points-off-turnovers as they forced 19 Brave miscues.</p>
